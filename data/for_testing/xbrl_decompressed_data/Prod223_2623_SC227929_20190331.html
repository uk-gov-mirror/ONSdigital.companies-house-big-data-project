Report of the Trustees
For The Year Ended 31st March 2019

The trustees who are also directors of the charity for the purposes of the Companies Act 2006, present their report with
the financial statements of the charity for the year ended 31st March 2019. The trustees have adopted the provisions of
Accounting and Reporting by Charities: Statement of Recommended Practice applicable to charities preparing their
accounts in accordance with the Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland (FRS 102)
(effective 1 January 2015).

Objectives and activities
Objectives and aims
The objectives are to be exercised following the principles of sustainable development (where sustainable development
means development which meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet
their own needs), namely:



a) To manage community land and associated assets for the benefit of the Community and the public in
general,with a particular emphasis, but not exclusively, on the conservation of Scotland's natural heritage and the
conservation, restoration and improvement of woodlands in and around Grantown on Spey;



b) To advance the education of the public generally but particularly the education of young people within the
Community concerning the local wildlife, conservation and preservation of the natural and cultural heritage of
the area;



c) To provide, or assist in providing, recreational facilities, and/or organising recreational activities, which will
be available to members of the Community and public at large with the object of improving the conditions of life
of the Community;

d) To promote, establish and operate other schemes of a charitable nature for the benefit of the Community.

Achievement and performance
Charitable activities
The woods continue to be in a generally healthy condition and enjoyed by people from Grantown and visitors. General
management activities in the past year included the removal of non-native tree species from the woods, clearance of
vegetation around paths and tracks and drain clearance. The Trust has undertaken some tree limbing and felling for the
safety of people using the woods or passing on the roads, and will continue to monitor the condition of trees in on our
promoted routes and neighbouring property. We have not undertaken any deer control in the past year, nor are we aware
of any illegal use of the woods or deliberate damage or vandalism in 2018/19.

The Board of Directors met regularly over the financial year 2018/19, spending the early part of the year identify and
prioritising work. Clearly, there are a range of interests and ideas about what's a priority, the maintenance of the
infrastructure that people use such as the paths and car parks, as well as the things could make the woods even more
enjoyable for its users. This takes time to plan and deliver properly, but key points from the year include:

1. Commissioned a path condition audit to identify a schedule for maintenance, repair and improvement of the promoted
paths. This will allow the Trust to get cost estimates for packages of path repair work and accurately target funding for
them.

2. Tested the use of a Truxor machine for removing reeds from the Kylintra Lochan. We see this as a pilot exercise, not
a commitment to maintain the loch free of vegetation.

3. Obtain quotes for replacement fences along the burn path at Kylintra so AWT can apply for funding to replace them.

4. Repaired potholes in and re-profile the Poorhouse Wood car park to improve drainage.

5. With advice from Plantlife Scotland, monitor the Anagach Twinflower patches and establish the management regime
that is most likely to maintain and improve their condition.

6. Appointed forestry consultants to advise on the review of our Long Term Forest Plan (LTFP) and on any significant
timber extraction.

Volunteering in Anagach
We undertook a significant number of practical tasks through the work of other volunteer groups including the Green
Gym, the Wolf Pack Cycle club and a team of staff from the Cairngorms National Park Authority.

Capercaillie
Capercaillie lek surveys were undertaken again in 2018, again showing male activity but without hens. The woods
continue to provide good physical habitat for capercaillie and are used year round, but there is little evidence of
breeding.

People Enjoying the Woods
The woods are enjoyed by many people in and around Grantown every day. The walks closest to Grantown are
particularly popular and we were delighted to install a new bench on the popular green route at the request of Grantown
residents in memory of a friend.
In addition to the informal use and enjoyment of the paths, mountain bike skills area, woods and wildlife, the woods
hosted many other organised activities in 2018/19 including:
- Use of the Grantown Primary School Nursery, Grantown Primary and use for orienteering and cross country running
by Grantown Grammar School.
- A Dramble walk as part of the Spirit of Speyside Whisky Festival;
- A Family Outdoor Play Programme on Tuesdays between August and October run by Roots and Shoots Highland;
- A 200 competitor orienteering event in November as well as informal orienteering training throughout the year.
- The popular Wolfpax Dirt Crit mountain bike event in November, regular visits by local cyclists and cycling clubs, as
well as visits from guided mountain bike groups using the skills area for tuition.

Given how heavily used the woods are throughout the year and at most times of day, we are pleased that we receive very
few complaints about other people's use of the woods.

Achievement and performance
Investment performance
The trustees are currently satisfied with the investment policy of holding reserve funds in an investment account. This
account is reviewed annually in an effort to achieve an optimum rate of interest.

Internal and external factors
Woodland management project funding is sourced where possible and the Trust tries to investigate all funding
opportunities for the benefit of the woodland's sustainability.

All projects are financially risk assessed and only undertaken once a realistic and manageable budget has been agreed.

The administration of the Trust can prove challenging throughout the year, with the lack of having any paid employees
and not having the maximum complement of voluntary Directors on the Board.

Financial review
Principal funding sources
During this financial year Anagach Woods Trust is pleased and fortunate to receive funding from private donations.

Reserves policy
It is Anagach Woods Trust's policy that unrestricted funds should be maintained to the equivalent of 6 months
expenditure in order for the Trust to continue functioning in a period of reduced income and the trustees believe that
they have established this level of reserve.

Structure, governance and management
Governing document
The charity is controlled by its governing document and constitutes a company limited by guarantee, as defined by the
Companies Act 2006.

Recruitment and appointment of new trustees
Members can nominate directors in advance of the AGM and they are then voted on for a three-year term. Elected
directors can stand for two consecutive terms before standing down for at least a year. The Board of Directors can also
co-opt directors to serve on the Board and they can be elected onto the Board at the next AGM, if they are a full member
of the Trust. Co-opted directors who are not full members need to be re-co-opted at or after the AGM and no more than
two co-opted directors can serve on the Board at any one time.

Related parties
The charity has a close relationship with Scottish National Heritage (SNH) and operates under a management agreement
with them.

1. Accounting policies

Basis of preparing the financial statements
The financial statements of the charitable company, which is a public benefit entity under FRS 102, have been
prepared in accordance with the Charities SORP (FRS 102) 'Accounting and Reporting by Charities: Statement
of Recommended Practice applicable to charities preparing their accounts in accordance with the Financial
Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland (FRS 102) (effective 1 January 2015)',
Financial Reporting Standard 102 'The Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of
Ireland' and the Companies Act 2006. The financial statements have been prepared under the historical cost
convention.

Income
All income is recognised in the Statement of Financial Activities once the charity has entitlement to the funds, it
is probable that the income will be received and the amount can be measured reliably.

Expenditure
Liabilities are recognised as expenditure as soon as there is a legal or constructive obligation committing the
charity to that expenditure, it is probable that a transfer of economic benefits will be required in settlement and
the amount of the obligation can be measured reliably. Expenditure is accounted for on an accruals basis and has
been classified under headings that aggregate all cost related to the category. Where costs cannot be directly
attributed to particular headings they have been allocated to activities on a basis consistent with the use of
resources.

Tangible fixed assets
Depreciation is provided at the following annual rates in order to write off each asset over its estimated useful
life.


Land - not provided

Equipment - 20% on cost

Taxation
The charity is exempt from corporation tax on its charitable activities.

Fund accounting
Unrestricted funds can be used in accordance with the charitable objectives at the discretion of the trustees.

Restricted funds can only be used for particular restricted purposes within the objects of the charity. Restrictions
arise when specified by the donor or when funds are raised for particular restricted purposes.
