REPORT OF THE TRUSTEES
FOR THE YEAR ENDED 30 JUNE 2019

The trustees who are also directors of the charity for the purposes of the Companies Act 2006, present their report with
the financial statements of the charity for the year ended 30 June 2019. The trustees have adopted the provisions of
Accounting and Reporting by Charities: Statement of Recommended Practice applicable to charities preparing their
accounts in accordance with the Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland (FRS 102)
(effective 1 January 2015).

OBJECTIVES AND ACTIVITIES
Objectives and aims
The charity's objects are:

-relief of sickness of people with Adrenoleukodystrophy (ALD), Adrenomyeloneuropathy (AMN) and other
leukodystrophies and their parents and carers;
-to promote medical research into Adrenoleukodystrophy, Adrenomyeloneuropathy and other leukodystrophies
and the publication of useful results of such research for the benefit of the public;
-to advance the education of the public in Adrenoleukodystrophy, Adrenomyeloneuropathy and other
leukodystrophies.

The trustees have referred to the guidance in the Charity Commission's general guidance on public benefit when
reviewing the charity's aims and objectives in planning its future activities. In particular, they have considered how
planned activities will contribute to the aims and objectives they have set.

ACHIEVEMENT AND PERFORMANCE
Fundraising
Alex TLC uses its own staff and volunteers to develop and implement internal fundraising campaigns and events. We are
a member of the Fundraising Regulator and the Fundraising Preference Service and received no complaints during this
period. We do not employ third parties to deliver fundraising activities and utilise a small budget to boost social media
activities and optimise reach. We provide communications support and supply promotional materials to external
fundraisers. All campaigns are monitored carefully to ensure they are not unreasonably intrusive, persistent or coercive.

Organisation
Our largest achievement was our successful expansion to support sufferers of all genetic leukodystrophies and
subsequent name change to Alex, The Leukodystrophy Charity. This was supported wholeheartedly by medical
professionals and existing beneficiaries and will bring us increased exposure to new avenues of support. This has shifted
the focus of our work from adrenoleukodystrophy (the most common of the leukodystrophies) to the similar outcomes
faced by the majority of those affected by leukodystrophy.

The Board and Senior Management Team were able to work more effectively due to reporting and strategic changes
implemented from the previous period. As part of the Lloyds funding received, we accessed mentoring in two areas,
finance and communications, from the Lloyds Enhance Programme. These were key in the decision to employ a Chief
Finance Officer and experienced Communications Manager in part time roles towards the end of the period.

We built an impressive portfolio of case studies and, along with an improved and ambitious communications strategy
have seen initial increases in digital engagement which we hope to translate into increased income over the next period.

Measures to optimise retail profits showed ongoing improvement with profits improved by 13% from the previous year.

Successful grant applications for specific projects included Global's Make Some Noise, Breaking Down Barriers,
Genetic Disorders UK and we sustained existing corporate support from bluebird bio and Minoryx Therapeutics.
Voluntary income increased slightly from the previous year.

We were asked to assist with providing distribution services for another charity which has translated into a regular
source of income.

ACHIEVEMENT AND PERFORMANCE


Charitable Objectives
The launch of Alex, The Leukodystrophy Charity in May 2019 was well attended by 67 attendees including medical
professionals, representatives from peer organisations and beneficiaries.

Over the course of the year staff were asked to present at 11 events in the UK and overseas, were invited to exhibit their
information at 5 rare disease and medical conferences and were involved in 55 meetings to develop specific projects.

We developed a new, comprehensive and dynamic website, updating both condition specific and condition management
information and initiated collaborative talks with peer charities.

Our support services welcomed 62 new beneficiaries and received 223 requests for phone and email assistance,
providing 38 home and advocacy visits.

Continued efforts to add adrenoleukodystrophy to the UK new born screening programme included working with
Genetic Alliance on a project to influence government to change the way the National Screening Committee reviews
applications. This resulted in nationwide press coverage and numerous news and television appearances by staff.

Projects to develop age appropriate children's literature and to ensure our information is more accessible for hard to
reach communities were commenced.

We assisted with the recruitment for a pharmaceutical trial and developed a close working relationship with a peer
charity to promote health information to beneficiaries with related conditions.

Challenges
For this financial period, we identified three key issues where we failed to deliver against budgeted targets:

1. Our launch and rebrand exceeded budget expectations

2. Our new shop is yet to show profit - Potential for more significant retail profits were limited due to disappointing
performance from our new shop which we feel is largely due to visibility. Measures have been put in place to remedy
this. Reduced profits also meant we were unable to invest in a further Gift Aid till in this period as planned.

3. Unrestricted grant expectations for the year were not met - Grant fundraising as a whole has been disappointing but
aligns with the experience of other small charities. Measures were taken to recruit voluntary grant fundraisers with
limited success.

The funding for our Monitoring & Evaluation officer role ended and, and with no further funding available was not
continued following contract end in May. This does leave a significant gap and efforts to secure support for this role
were unsuccessful in this period but will continue.

There is a need for a supporting administrative role to allow senior staff the time to seek the funding necessary to meet
charitable objectives.

Future
Our future is dependent on increasing our reach and potential for securing sufficient funds. If successful, an ambitious
2019/20 budget and communications strategy will target significant increases in voluntary income and corporate support.
This will allow us to implement a wider range of support services, and also influence health and care services to provide
best practice treatment and care for patient and their families.

Further investment in Gift Aid tills will also optimise retail profits alongside strategies to attract more customers, sell
stock online and further reduce expenditure.

FINANCIAL REVIEW
Financial position
At the balance sheet date, the charity had unrestricted funds of 47,575 (2018: 38,627) and restricted funds of 54,226
(2018: 67,190).

Reserves policy
It is the policy of the charity that unrestricted funds which have not been designated for a specific use should be
maintained at a level the trustees feel are sufficient to cover the running costs of the charity for a period of
approximately 3 months (160,000). The trustees are aware that the current level is still below this and that unrestricted
funds at the end of the year were unable to fully cover expenditure liabilities.

However, reserve levels have increased by 23% on the previous year and will continue to do so utilising improved
financial reporting structures, which have been instrumental in enabling us to identify key areas of concern.

FUTURE PLANS
For 2019/20 we plan to:

- Hold our first Community Weekend as Alex, The Leukodystrophy Charity and welcome attendees affected
by, and with an interest in, all genetic leukodystrophies
- Develop and test outputs for age appropriate children's information
- Translate our website into Spanish and four other European languages
- Complete an audit of our database to determine demographics and assess the accessibility needs of hard to
reach beneficiaries
- Initiate work with peer organisations to further influence how new born screening is assessed for rare
conditions in the UK
- Employ a fundraising and administration officer to support senior staff
- Install at least one additional Gift Aid till in our charity shop business

STRUCTURE, GOVERNANCE AND MANAGEMENT
Governing document
The charity is controlled by its governing document, a deed of trust, and constitutes a limited company, limited by
guarantee, as defined by the Companies Act 2006.

Recruitment and appointment of new trustees
The Charity seeks to appoint trustees who have relevant and often specialist knowledge. This has always included
ensuring that those directly affected by the condition, such as parents, are among the trustees. This is complemented by
appointing as trustees those with complementary skills, e.g. legal, finance, administration or relevant professional
experience and knowledge.

None of the trustees has any beneficial interest in the company. All of the trustees are members of the company and
guarantee to contribute 1 in the event of a winding up.

Key management remuneration
Key personnel remuneration is reviewed annually alongside performance appraisals within an agreed pay structure and
applied using the same approach to pay and reward as for all employees. Any significant increases are approved by the
Board of Trustees and reviewed within the constraints of the budget.

Risk management
The trustees have assessed the major risks to which the charity is exposed, and are satisfied that systems are in place to
mitigate exposure to the major risks.

1. ACCOUNTING POLICIES

Basis of preparing the financial statements
The financial statements of the charitable company, which is a public benefit entity under FRS 102, have been
prepared in accordance with the Charities SORP (FRS 102) 'Accounting and Reporting by Charities: Statement
of Recommended Practice applicable to charities preparing their accounts in accordance with the Financial
Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland (FRS 102) (effective 1 January 2015)',
Financial Reporting Standard 102 'The Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of
Ireland' and the Companies Act 2006. The financial statements have been prepared under the historical cost
convention.

Alex, The Leukodystrophy Charity is a charitable company, limited by guarantee, incorporated in England and
Wales. The registered office is 45 Peckham High Street, London, SE15 5EB.

The financial statements are prepared in sterling, which is the functional currency of the charitable company.
Monetary amounts in these financial statements are rounded to the nearest .

Income
All income is recognised in the Statement of Financial Activities once the charity has entitlement to the funds, it
is probable that the income will be received and the amount can be measured reliably.

Donations and similar incoming resources are included in the year in which they are receivable, which is when
the charity becomes entitled to the resource.

Grant income is recognised at the point when the charity becomes legally entitled to the income and may be
deferred in accordance with the terms of the funders at the time of receipt.

Fundraising income is recognised when the charity becomes legally entitled to the income and the event to which
it relates has taken place and includes sales of shop goods and is recognised at the point of sale.

Investment income is recognised as it is received.

Expenditure
Liabilities are recognised as expenditure as soon as there is a legal or constructive obligation committing the
charity to that expenditure, it is probable that a transfer of economic benefits will be required in settlement and
the amount of the obligation can be measured reliably. Expenditure is accounted for on an accruals basis and has
been classified under headings that aggregate all cost related to the category. Where costs cannot be directly
attributed to particular headings they have been allocated to activities on a basis consistent with the use of
resources.

Charitable expenditure comprises those costs incurred by the charity in the delivery of its activities and services
for its beneficiaries.

Grants offered subject to conditions which have not been met at the year end date are noted as a commitment but
not accrued as expenditure.

Allocation and apportionment of costs
Support costs are those functions that assist the work of the charity but do not directly undertake charitable
activities. Support costs include back office and governance costs which support Beneficiary Services,
Awareness and Education and Research. These costs have been allocated and apportioned to the various
charitable activities. A percentage basis, based on direct expenditure incurred has been used to apportion the
support costs to the charitable activities.
