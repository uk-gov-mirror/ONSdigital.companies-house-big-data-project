The directors present their strategic report for the year ended 30 June 2019.

BUSINESS STRATEGY AND OBJECTIVES

The company has been established to provide investment advice to private individuals, pension funds and charities.

The company has approximately 100 wealthy clients and its principal objective is to provide a highly personalised level
of service thus retaining its existing client base and also attracting new clients to the business many of whom are
referrals from existing clients. The company's revenue is derived from a combination of management fees and charges
on transactions.

REVIEW OF BUSINESS
Turnover levels for the year have increased by 35,437 from 826,027 to 861,464. Administrative expenses have fallen
by 9,068, and the overall profitability of the business has increased by 35,071. Operating margin increased slightly to
39%.

PRINCIPAL RISKS AND UNCERTAINTIES
Risk management

The firm is governed by its director who determines its business and risk strategies. The director is the owner of the
business and has extensive experience in the provision of investment advice and company procedures and activities.

The director has established risk management procedures in order to ensure that the company has effective systems
and controls in place to identify, monitor and manage risks arising in the business.

The director manages the company's risks through a framework of policies and procedures having regard to relevant
laws, standards, principles and rules (including FCA principles and rules) with the aim of operating a defined and
transparent risk management framework. These policies and procedures are reviewed regularly and updated as
required.

The firm is relatively small with an operational infrastructure appropriate to its size.

Where material risks are identified, the director considers mitigation strategies and the financial impact of these risks as
part of the business planning and capital management. The director also assesses whether the amount of regulatory
capital held by the firm is adequate.

Management accounts that demonstrate the continued adequacy of the firm's regulatory capital are prepared on a
quarterly basis or as required.

The specific risks applicable to the firm are credit, market, business and operational risks.

Credit risk

Credit risk is the risk that a party will default on a financial agreement. The firm is exposed to credit risk in respect of
investment management fees due from clients and deposits held with UK authorised banks.

The company has very low credit risk. Fees are paid under approved agreements on the client accounts held by
Pershing Securities Limited, who provide the back office function. There has been no history of any complaint as to
charges or fees. The company has never had a bad debtor.

The financial controller is responsible for ensuring that the firm's records are in agreement with those of the bank. The
bank reconciliations are reviewed on a quarterly basis by third party accountants.


Market risk

Market risk is the risk that arises from fluctuations in values of, or income from, assets or risks associated with interest
rates or exchange rates. The firm does not run a trading book and as such only has exposure to debtor and bank
balances held in currencies other than sterling. The company does not hold currencies other than sterling held in
banks.

The investment advice that the company provides to its clients is centred around the use of quoted and regulated
collective investment funds owned and managed by third party asset management companies. The only significant risk
is considered to be a long-term downturn in the market generally. In such circumstances the profits of the company
would fall and the dividend available to its owner would reduce. The overhead cost base of the company is relatively
small. The remuneration policy makes it clear that if necessary drawings from the business by the owner will be
reduced to ensure the ongoing profitability of the business. The assessment reflects the experience of the company
within its market over recent years.

Business risk

Business risk is the exposure to uncertainty in the macroeconomic environment.

This is identified as a significant loss of funds. It is noted above under Market Risk that the company's fixed overheads
are very low and therefore the impact of such an eventuality does not give rise to an additional capital requirement. It is
noted that the company has seen growth in its funds during its entire history. The company's trading performance is
strong with well managed and diversified investment performance.

The director conducts a formal assessment of the business risk to which the firm is exposed on an annual basis, though
given the size and nature of the firm no separate risk management function is considered necessary in respect of the
firm's own balance sheet. Matters arising from the review are considered and mitigating or remedial action is taken
where appropriate.

Operational risk is the risk of loss arising from inadequate or failed internal processes, people and systems or from
external events.

The firm mitigates operational risk through the establishment of robust systems, controls and audit procedures. The
director conducts a formal assessment of the operational risk to which the firm is exposed on an annual basis.
Moreover operational risk is considered during regular management meetings.

The company is reliant on key personnel. Although risk in relation to these staff is minimised by their commitment to the
business, arrangements have been put in place for the business to operate in the short term in case of their
incapacitation.

FUTURE DEVELOPMENTS
The company intends to build on its current position through organic growth of the customer base, while maintaining
administrative costs at a similar level.

2. ACCOUNTING POLICIES

Basis of preparing the financial statements
These financial statements have been prepared in accordance with Financial Reporting Standard 102 "The Financial Reporting Standard applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland" and the Companies Act 2006. The financial statements have been prepared under the historical cost convention.

Turnover
Turnover is measured at the fair value of the consideration received or receivable, excluding discounts, rebates,
value added tax and other sales taxes.

The following criteria must also be met before revenue is recognised:

Annual management fees, third party nominee and administration costs are recognised when they are earned by
the Company under the appropriate contractual arrangements.

Advisor charges are recognised on the completion date of the purchase or sale to which they relate.

Tangible fixed assets
Depreciation is provided at the following annual rates in order to write off each asset over its estimated useful life.
Equipment - 33% on straight line basis

Taxation
Taxation for the year comprises current and deferred tax. Tax is recognised in the Statement of Comprehensive
Income, except to the extent that it relates to items recognised in other comprehensive income or directly in
equity.

Current or deferred taxation assets and liabilities are not discounted.

Current tax is recognised at the amount of tax payable using the tax rates and laws that have been enacted or
substantively enacted by the balance sheet date.

Deferred tax
Deferred tax is recognised in respect of all timing differences that have originated but not reversed at the
balance sheet date.

Timing differences arise from the inclusion of income and expenses in tax assessments in periods different from
those in which they are recognised in financial statements. Deferred tax is measured using tax rates and laws
that have been enacted or substantively enacted by the year end and that are expected to apply to the reversal
of the timing difference.

Unrelieved tax losses and other deferred tax assets are recognised only to the extent that it is probable that they
will be recovered against the reversal of deferred tax liabilities or other future taxable profits.

Pension costs and other post-retirement benefits
The company operates a defined contribution pension scheme. Contributions payable to the company's pension
scheme are charged to profit or loss in the period to which they relate.
